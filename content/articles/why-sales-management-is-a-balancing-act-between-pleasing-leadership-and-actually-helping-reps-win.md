0px;">Make the expectations on both sides written, not assumed</h3><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 22px; padding: 0px;">Most of the friction in this balancing act comes from expectations that were never written down. Leadership thinks they asked for one thing. The rep heard another. The manager is stuck in the middle absorbing the blame for the gap. The fix is an Accountabilities Document for every role on the team, including yours. Put on paper what each person owns, what they're measured on, and what they can expect from the layer above them. When the standard is written, you stop managing by mood and start managing by agreement. Both directions get clearer, and the politics lose most of their oxygen.</p><h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-top: 28px; margin-bottom: 10px; padding: 0px;">Run the meeting cadence so it serves the reps, not just the forecast</h3><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 22px; padding: 0px;">As a team grows, managers drown in meetings and context switching, and the calendar quietly fills with sessions that serve reporting instead of selling. If a meeting only exists to make the layer above you comfortable, it's stealing time from the people who carry your quota. Protect their selling time like it's your own income, because it is.</p><hr style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 36px 0px; padding: 0px; border-right: none; border-bottom: none; border-left: none; border-image: initial; border-top-style: solid; border-top-color: rgb(226, 230, 237);"><h2 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-top: 40px; margin-bottom: 14px; padding: 0px;">What It Costs When You Get the Balance Wrong</h2><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 22px; padding: 0px;">Tip too far toward pleasing leadership and your reps feel abandoned. That's the trap of founder-led sales.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 22px; padding: 0px;">A full-time VP of Sales is a $250K-plus bet that you may not be ready to make, and the wrong hire sets you back a year.
