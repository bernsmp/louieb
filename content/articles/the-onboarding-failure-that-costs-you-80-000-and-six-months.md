The offer letter covers compensation, start date, and maybe a job description. It protects you legally. You need both, and most $1M–$10M ARR companies have neither.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">Without a structured coaching cadence, even reps who start strong will plateau, drift, or quietly start looking for their next job. Every active opportunity reviewed for next action, expected close, and risk. No stage inflation.</p><h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-top: 28px; margin-bottom: 12px; padding: 0px;">Monthly: One-on-One Development</h3><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">Once per month, per rep. If you are still running founder-led sales while trying to build this infrastructure, something important will get cut short.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">In almost every case, it is the onboarding plan. And you wonder why the same pattern keeps repeating with every new hire.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">A Fractional Sales Leader builds the entire onboarding infrastructure before your next hire starts. The Position Contract is written. The Sales Playbook is documented. The 30-day plan is sequenced. The coaching cadence is scheduled.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">Your next rep walks into a complete system. They do not have to figure out anything. They just have to show up and execute the proven and repeatable process that is already in place.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">That is how you get to first sale in 30 days instead of 90.</p><hr style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 36px 0px; padding: 0px; border-right: none; border-bottom: none; border-left: none; border-image: initial; border-top-style: solid; border-top-color: rgb(226, 232, 240);"><h2 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-top: 40px; margin-bottom: 16px; padding: 0px; letter-spacing: -0.015em;">Frequently Asked Questions</h2><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 28px; padding: 0px;"><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 700;">Q: How long should a formal structured onboarding process last?</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.7;">Thirty days for the intensive structured plan, but active ramp support continues through Day 90. The first 30 days build the foundation. Days 31–90 are independent execution with a coaching cadence fully in place. By Day 90, a rep should be self-directed and fully accountable to the activity metrics in their Position Contract.</p></div><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 28px; padding: 0px;"><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 700;">Q: What if I do not have recorded calls to use as Gold Calls yet?</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.7;">Start recording immediately — most CRMs and dialers include call recording. The absence of recordings is not a reason to skip this step; it is a reason to fix the gap today. In the interim, run live role-plays as a substitute. The practice of reviewing a successful call and naming exactly what worked is the behavioral principle. The recording is the tool, not the point.</p></div><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 28px; padding: 0px;"><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 700;">Q: Should I build the sales rep onboarding plan before or after I make the hire?</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.7;">Before. Always before. Building it after you hire means your new rep waits in limbo while you scramble to put it together — which is the opposite of the fast ramp you are trying to create. More importantly, building the onboarding plan forces you to document your own sales process first. That documentation is the foundation of everything that follows.</p></div><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 28px; padding: 0px;"><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 700;">Q: My last rep said the onboarding process was too rigid. How do I handle that pushback?</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.7;">Treat the Sales Playbook and onboarding process as a floor, not a ceiling. Require the rep to follow the proven and repeatable process for at least 60 days — long enough to understand why it works. After that, they can propose specific modifications based on what they are actually seeing in the field. Freedom to deviate in Week 1 is not independence. It is an invitation to wing it at your expense.</p></div><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 28px; padding: 0px;"><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 700;">Q: What is the single most important thing I can do right now to improve my onboarding process?</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.7;">Write down your own sales process this week. Not a formal document — a plain honest description of how you personally close deals. What do you say on the first call? What questions do you ask to qualify? Without it, every new rep starts from zero.
