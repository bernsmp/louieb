Bottom-up, by letting the best operator emerge naturally.</p><blockquote style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-top: 28px; margin-bottom: 28px; padding: 14px 20px; border-left-width: 4px; border-left-color: rgb(232, 97, 10); border-radius: 0px 6px 6px 0px;"><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-bottom: 20px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.6; margin-top: 0px !important; margin-right: 0px !important; margin-left: 0px !important;">The rep who outperforms — and helps their peer do the same — is your future sales manager. It does not require manufactured pressure or aggressive competition. It just requires visibility into each other's activity.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">When Rep A sees Rep B booked four discovery calls on Tuesday, Rep A books five on Wednesday. When Rep B sees Rep A close a deal they had been chasing for six weeks, Rep B works harder to close their own stalled opportunities.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">This is human nature. We are wired to respond to peers, not just managers.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">A single rep has only their own past performance as a benchmark. That is a weak motivational engine. Two reps create a dynamic where they pull each other toward higher performance simply by being visible to each other.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">The founder who hires two reps typically gets more total output than two individual reps would produce independently. That is the competition effect — and it is free.</p><hr style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 36px 0px; padding: 0px; border-right: none; border-bottom: none; border-left: none; border-image: initial; border-top-style: solid; border-top-color: rgb(226, 232, 240);"><h2 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-top: 40px; margin-bottom: 16px; padding: 0px; letter-spacing: -0.015em;">The Non-Negotiable Prerequisite: Close 10 Deals Yourself First</h2><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">Before any of this applies to you, there is one requirement that cannot be skipped.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">Do not hire any salesperson, one or two, until you have personally closed at least 10 deals and written down what happened in each one.</p><h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-top: 28px; margin-bottom: 12px; padding: 0px;">Why This Is Not Optional</h3><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">If you hand a new rep a vague product description and tell them to "start selling," you have not hired a salesperson. You have hired a very expensive experiment.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">Every rep you hire without a documented process will spend months figuring out what to say, how to qualify, how to handle objections, and how to close. margin: 0px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.65;"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 600;">Your objection responses:</span>&nbsp;The 5–10 objections that come up in almost every deal, and your proven responses</li><li style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.65;"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 600;">Your follow-up sequence:</span>&nbsp;What happens after the first call, the demo, the proposal</li></ul><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">Four sections. A competent rep can internalize it in a day and begin applying it in a week. Without it, they are winging it. And winging it is the most expensive thing your sales team can do.</p><hr style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 36px 0px; padding: 0px; border-right: none; border-bottom: none; border-left: none; border-image: initial; border-top-style: solid; border-top-color: rgb(226, 232, 240);"><h2 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-top: 40px; margin-bottom: 16px; padding: 0px; letter-spacing: -0.015em;">The Structured Hiring Process for Your First Two Reps</h2><h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-top: 28px; margin-bottom: 12px; padding: 0px;">Step 1: Write the Ideal Candidate Profile Before You Post Anything</h3><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">The job posting tells candidates what you need. The Ideal Candidate Profile tells&nbsp;<em style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">you</em>&nbsp;who to look for, with enough specificity to actually evaluate whether any given candidate is the right fit.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">The profile describes: the sales environment they must be comfortable in (early-stage, no brand recognition, outbound-heavy), the activities they will do every day, the metrics they will be held accountable to, and the traits that predict success in your specific culture. When your profile is specific, you attract genuine fits and naturally filter out candidates who will struggle.</p><h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-top: 28px; margin-bottom: 12px; padding: 0px;">Step 2: Run Both Searches in Parallel</h3><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">Interview for both positions simultaneously. This prevents the most common mistake in sales talent acquisition: settling for a B-plus candidate because you are too desperate to wait for an A.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">Recruiting sales reps from a position of urgency is the worst possible position to be in. Running parallel searches keeps your options open and your standards high.</p><h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-top: 28px; margin-bottom: 12px; padding: 0px;">Step 3: Start Both Reps Within Two Weeks of Each Other</h3><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">Stagger their start dates by no more than two weeks. The reason is simple: shared onboarding is dramatically more effective than solo onboarding.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">When two new reps go through structured onboarding together, they role-play together. They compare notes from early calls. They hold each other accountable through the ramp period. A rep who onboards alone either figures it out or does not. A rep who onboards with a peer almost always figures it out faster.</p><h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-top: 28px; margin-bottom: 12px; padding: 0px;">Step 4: Write the 30-60-90 Day Plan Before Day 1</h3><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">Define success in writing before either rep starts. By Day 30: product knowledge complete, CRM active, first 50 outbound touches made. By Day 60: first discovery calls booked, first qualified opportunities in the pipeline. By Day 90: first late-stage deals with a realistic path to close in month four.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">When performance expectations are written down before someone starts, there is no ambiguity. And ambiguity is exactly where underperformance hides.</p><hr style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 36px 0px; padding: 0px; border-right: none; border-bottom: none; border-left: none; border-image: initial; border-top-style: solid; border-top-color: rgb(226, 232, 240);"><h2 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-top: 40px; margin-bottom: 16px; padding: 0px; letter-spacing: -0.015em;">The One Mistake That Undoes Everything</h2><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">Founders who hire two salespeople sometimes make one critical error that negates every benefit described above.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">They mentally check out of sales the moment the reps start.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">The moment you hire two reps, you assume they have it covered. You pivot to product, fundraising, partnerships, whatever is next on your list. But in the first 90 days, your reps need you actively present. They need to watch you close a deal. They need you on calls as a silent observer. They need a debrief after something goes wrong.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px;">You are not handing off sales on Day 1. You are transitioning it. That transition requires 90–120 days of your genuine involvement.</p><blockquote style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-top: 28px; margin-bottom: 28px; padding: 14px 20px; border-left-width: 4px; border-left-color: rgb(232, 97, 10); border-radius: 0px 6px 6px 0px;"><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-bottom: 20px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.6; margin-top: 0px !important; margin-right: 0px !important; margin-left: 0px !important;">Handing off sales to new reps without a transition period is like handing someone the wheel of a moving car and jumping out the door.</p></blockquote><hr style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 36px 0px; padding: 0px; border-right: none; border-bottom: none; border-left: none; border-image: initial; border-top-style: solid; border-top-color: rgb(226, 232, 240);"><h2 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin-top: 40px; margin-bottom: 16px; padding: 0px; letter-spacing: -0.015em;">Frequently Asked Questions</h2><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 28px; padding: 0px;"><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 700;">Q: What if I genuinely cannot afford two salespeople right now?</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.7;">Hire one, but build the entire system as if you have two. Create the written Sales Playbook, the structured onboarding plan, the Position Contracts, and the accountability framework now. The moment budget exists for the second rep, you are ready to hire immediately. Do not wait until you are desperate to build the infrastructure — that is when bad hires happen.</p></div><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 28px; padding: 0px;"><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 700;">Q: Should I hire two BDRs, two Account Executives, or one of each?</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.7;">For most $1M–$5M ARR companies, hire two full-cycle reps. These are people who can both prospect and close. At this stage, you do not have the operational infrastructure to run a split BDR/AE model effectively. Keep it simple. Hire people who own the entire sales motion from first outbound touch to signed contract.</p></div><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 28px; padding: 0px;"><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 700;">Q: What if one rep is clearly outperforming the other early in the ramp?</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.7;">Good. That is exactly what the system is supposed to produce. The stronger rep will push the weaker one. If the weaker rep is not improving meaningfully by Month 4, you have objective data for a performance conversation,&nbsp; and your sales function is not disrupted while you have it, because you still have one producing rep.</p></div><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 28px; padding: 0px;"><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 700;">Q: How do I prevent the two reps from competing destructively?</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 20px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.7;">Define territories, verticals, or lead source ownership clearly from Day 1.
