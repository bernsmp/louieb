They do not need infrastructure to function — they create it.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 22px; padding: 0px;">A&nbsp;<strong style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">Driver</strong>&nbsp;optimizes a machine that already exists. They have improved close rates, scaled teams, refined comp plans, and accelerated pipelines. They are excellent at making a good system better. They have spent most of their careers at companies where someone else had already built the foundation.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 22px; padding: 0px;">At $1M–$10M ARR, with no documented sales process, no functional CRM, and a founder still closing most deals, you need a Builder. In every VP of Sales interview. Do not skip any because the conversation is going well or the candidate seems impressive. Impressive in a room is not the same as capable in the field.</p><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 28px 0px; padding: 24px 28px; border-color: rgb(226, 230, 237); border-style: solid; border-width: 1px; border-image: none 100% / 1 / 0 stretch; border-radius: 8px;"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 12px; padding: 4px 12px; display: inline-block; font-weight: 700; letter-spacing: 0.08em; text-transform: uppercase; border-radius: 4px;">Question 1</span><h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 16px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.4;">Describe the last time you built a sales process completely from scratch — no existing playbook, no functional CRM, no documented system.</h3><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 16px 0px 6px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 700; letter-spacing: 0.07em; text-transform: uppercase;">Why You Ask It</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px; padding: 0px;">This is the most important question on this list. You cannot learn them on your first day at a $3M ARR company on your dime.</p><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 8px 0px 0px; padding: 0px; display: grid; grid-template-columns: 1fr 1fr; gap: 12px;"><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 12px 14px; border-radius: 6px; line-height: 1.5; border: 1px solid rgb(165, 214, 167);"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 6px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 700; letter-spacing: 0.08em; text-transform: uppercase; display: block;">Strong Answer</span>Describes a specific company at your stage or earlier, names the constraints, and explains what they built or did to work around them. The story involves resourcefulness, not resources.</div><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 12px 14px; border-radius: 6px; line-height: 1.5; border: 1px solid rgb(239, 154, 154);"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 6px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 700; letter-spacing: 0.08em; text-transform: uppercase; display: block;">Weak Answer</span>"My smallest company was around $40M ARR when I joined" — or any answer that reveals they have only operated where infrastructure already existed. The skills do not transfer the way they think they will.</div></div></div><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 28px 0px; padding: 24px 28px; border-color: rgb(226, 230, 237); border-style: solid; border-width: 1px; border-image: none 100% / 1 / 0 stretch; border-radius: 8px;"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 12px; padding: 4px 12px; display: inline-block; font-weight: 700; letter-spacing: 0.08em; text-transform: uppercase; border-radius: 4px;">Question 4</span><h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 16px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.4;">Show me a Sales Playbook you have written personally. Or: a description so vague it is clear they are describing a concept, not something they actually built.</div></div></div><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 28px 0px; padding: 24px 28px; border-color: rgb(226, 230, 237); border-style: solid; border-width: 1px; border-image: none 100% / 1 / 0 stretch; border-radius: 8px;"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 12px; padding: 4px 12px; display: inline-block; font-weight: 700; letter-spacing: 0.08em; text-transform: uppercase; border-radius: 4px;">Question 5</span><h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 16px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.4;">Tell me about a sales hire you made that did not work out. What happened, and what did you do wrong?</h3><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 16px 0px 6px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 700; letter-spacing: 0.07em; text-transform: uppercase;">Why You Ask It</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px; padding: 0px;">How a candidate handles this question tells you more about their character than almost anything else on this list. You are not looking for a perfect track record. You are looking for self-awareness, honesty, and the ability to take ownership of a failure without deflecting blame onto the person they hired.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px; padding: 0px;">A candidate who cannot name a single bad hire is either inexperienced or unwilling to be honest with you. The blame goes sideways. Nothing was their fault. Nothing changed.</div></div></div><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 28px 0px; padding: 24px 28px; border-color: rgb(226, 230, 237); border-style: solid; border-width: 1px; border-image: none 100% / 1 / 0 stretch; border-radius: 8px;"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 12px; padding: 4px 12px; display: inline-block; font-weight: 700; letter-spacing: 0.08em; text-transform: uppercase; border-radius: 4px;">Question 6</span><h3 style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 16px; padding: 0px; line-height: 1.4;">Our founder is still closing most deals. What is your plan for changing that, and what do you need from the founder to make it work?</h3><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 16px 0px 6px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 700; letter-spacing: 0.07em; text-transform: uppercase;">Why You Ask It</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px; padding: 0px;">This question does two things. It tells you whether they understand the real problem — founder dependency, not just weak reps. And it tells you whether they have done this specific transition before. Getting a founder out of the day-to-day close is a distinct skill. It requires extracting and documenting the founder's institutional knowledge, building reps who can replicate it, and managing the founder's instinct to jump back in when a deal gets complicated.</p><p style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px; padding: 0px;">A candidate who has never done this will give you a generic answer about "empowering the team." That is the hardest part and most founders resist it."</div><div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 12px 14px; border-radius: 6px; line-height: 1.5; border: 1px solid rgb(239, 154, 154);"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 6px; padding: 0px; font-weight: 700; letter-spacing: 0.08em; text-transform: uppercase; display: block;">Weak Answer</span>"I will get the team confident so that you can step back."
