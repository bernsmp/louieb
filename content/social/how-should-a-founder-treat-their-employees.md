# LinkedIn Post

Treat your employees exactly as you want them to treat your best customer.

That's the whole formula. It's worked every time I've used it.

Here's why it matters more than any pep talk or ping-pong table.

The way you treat your salespeople is the ceiling on how they treat your customers. You can't hand a customer something you never handed the person selling to them.

If your salespeople feel rushed, second-guessed, and kept in the dark, that's exactly how your customers will feel on the other end of the call.

So it runs as a circle.

Treat your people well. They treat your customers well. Your customers buy. Then you get to reward your people.

Win. Win. Win. Win.

The opposite circle is just as real. Squeeze your team and they'll squeeze your customers, and your revenue will tell the story before you do.

For founders trying to get out of founder-led sales, this is the part nobody says out loud: you can't hand your customers to someone you never really invested in.

How you treat your first salesperson decides whether you'll still be closing every deal yourself at $8M ARR.

And treating people well isn't the soft option. It's clarity, fair pay, weekly coaching, and a written standard you hold everyone to, including yourself. That's demanding.

Soft is leaving the target undefined and hoping people read your mind.

Get the way you treat your team right, and the customers take care of themselves.
