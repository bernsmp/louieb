## LinkedIn Post (300–400 words)

The objection I hear most about hiring a Fractional Sales Leader is the wrong objection.

It goes like this:

"How can they lead my team if they're only here 25 hours a week? I'll end up paying for a leader and still doing the managing myself."

The concern is real. The framing is wrong.

The "always available" full-time sales manager is usually the symptom of the problem you are trying to solve — not the cure.

Here is why.

When a manager is on-site five days a week, every rep brings every question to that manager. Pricing exceptions. Deal terms. Whether a prospect is qualified. What to say to a stalled deal. The manager becomes the bottleneck for every decision the team should be making.

The team never develops independent judgment. The pipeline runs through one person's head. And you, the founder, are one resignation away from losing the entire sales function with the person carrying it.

A real Fractional Sales Leader does not solve that with more hours. They solve it with four artifacts.

A weekly operating cadence — fixed times, fixed agendas, that the team runs whether the leader is on-site or not.

A written accountability document — what every rep is responsible for, measured against, and reviewed on.

A documented playbook — the written answer to the 80 percent of recurring sales questions, so the team stops interrupting the leader for things the playbook already answered.

A one-page decision authority map — who decides what, in writing, so deals do not stall waiting for someone.

That is leadership. Not desk hours.

The 25 on-site hours are when the high-leverage meetings happen — pipeline review, 1:1 coaching, deal strategy, hiring. The other five days, the team runs the cadence the leader built.

By month two, you should feel less involved in sales than you did at week one. If you feel busier, the system was not installed correctly.

Stop measuring leadership by hours on a calendar. Measure it by what the team does when the leader is not in the room.

If you've thought a Fractional Sales Leader can't really lead your team because they're only there 25 hours a week — you're focused on the wrong thing.

Leadership is not desk hours. It is the cadence, the accountability document, the playbook, and the decision map that run when the leader is not in the room.

A full-time sales manager who is "always available" usually makes the problem worse, not better. Every rep interrupts them for every question. The team never learns to decide anything on its own.

A good fractional leader installs systems. The team runs them. You stop being the manager.
