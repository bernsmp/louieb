# LinkedIn Post

Your pipeline stages aren't folders. They're a stopwatch you forgot to read.

Most founders I meet at $1M to $10M can name their stages. Almost none of them can tell me how many days a normal deal spends in each one.

That's the gap. And it's costing them a quarter at a time.

Here's the part nobody connects: your sales cycle length is just the sum of the time deals sit in each stage. Cycle length is also the denominator in your velocity formula. So your stages are where revenue speeds up or stalls. Nowhere else.

Add up the average time-in-stage and one stage will be fat. That's your bottleneck. Maybe deals fly through Discovery and then sit in Proposal for a month because your pricing is confusing or your champion can't sell it internally. You can't fix what you've never measured.

The same data does something else for you. A deal sitting in Proposal for 45 days when your average is 14 isn't a live deal. It's a ghost that hasn't told you yet. Time-in-stage makes it age out and flag itself, so your forecast stops lying to you.

And it ends the oldest argument in sales. When one rep's deals stall where everyone else's move fine, that's coaching. When every rep stalls in the same stage, that's process. The clock tells you which.

You don't need a new tool for any of this. The timestamps are already in your CRM. You just have to look.

The founders who win the next year won't be the ones with the most deals. They'll be the ones who know exactly where their deals slow down, and fix that one stage first.
