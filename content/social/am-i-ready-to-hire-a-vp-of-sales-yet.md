# Am I Ready to Hire a VP of Sales Yet?

## LinkedIn Post

A VP of Sales doesn't build your sales engine. They scale one that already runs.

That one sentence kills most of the VP hires I see fail.

Roughly 70% of first-time VP of Sales hires don't last eighteen months. Founders blame the hire. It's almost never the hire.

It's that they brought in a $400K operator to run an operation that didn't exist yet. No playbook. No defined customer. A founder still closing every deal that mattered.

You can't scale a sales motion that lives only in your head.

So before you ask "can I afford a VP," ask the better question: would a VP have anything to run on day one?

Here's what "ready" actually looks like. You're out of founder-led sales, deals close when you're not in the room. You've got a defined Ideal Customer Profile, not "anyone with a budget." You've got a written playbook someone else can run. You've got a forecast you'd actually take to the board. And at least one rep has closed on the process by following it, not by being you.

If you can check all five, hire the VP. They'll scale a machine that already works.

If you can't, hiring a VP to go build those five is the most expensive experiment a founder can run. They weren't hired to find out whether your sales model works. They were hired to grow one that already does.

Get the foundation right first, and the right VP becomes a multiplier instead of a casualty.
