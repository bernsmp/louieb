# LinkedIn Post

Ask a Fractional Sales Leader to "see how things go for a few months" and you've already lost.

That's not fractional sales management. That's a retainer with a friendlier name.

Real fractional sales management runs on a 90-day plan. A written one. Deliverables for each 30-day block, the metrics that prove progress, and something concrete you own at the end.

Here's why the 90-day window matters.

It's long enough to install real infrastructure: a documented playbook, a pipeline you can forecast from, an Accountabilities Document for every seat.

And it's short enough that you can measure whether it worked before you commit to the next cycle. No year-long leash. No hoping.

So when you're evaluating a Fractional Sales Leader, the test is simple. Ask to see the plan.

If they can describe your first 90 days in concrete deliverables, they're selling you an outcome.

If they can only talk about hours and "getting to know the business," they're selling you time. Pass.

The other thing founders miss: the point isn't to make you dependent on the fractional leader. It's the opposite. By day 90 you should have a weekly rhythm that runs without them. The plan installs the machine. The rhythm keeps it running after the engagement ends.

The best money you'll spend at $1M–$10M ARR isn't on someone's time. It's on a plan you can hold in your hand and check against the calendar.

Build the machine first. Then decide who runs it.
