# How Do I Calculate Quota for My AEs?

## LinkedIn Post

"What quota should I give my AE?"

Founders ask me this constantly. And they ask it backwards.

Quota isn't a knob you turn. It's step TWO of a five-step model. Skip the other four and you'll either starve your growth or blow up your budget. I've watched both happen to good companies.

Here's the math most founders never run:

→ You want +$10M in net-new revenue next year.
→ Deals are ~$25K. A $600K quota is reasonable for that deal size.
→ $10M ÷ $600K = 16 AEs just to do the closing.
→ Add 4 SDRs, 1 VP, 2 managers = 22 heads.
→ Now divide by a 0.75 yield factor. Industry-average attainment is ~74% (CaptivateIQ), and the average SaaS rep takes 5+ months to ramp (Bridge Group).

= 30 sales professionals to net that $10M. Even with a healthy quota.

Most founders imagine a dozen reps. The gap between what they picture and what the math requires is the #1 reason growth plans miss. Not talent. Arithmetic.

And here's the part nobody warns you about: just the act of scaling, the management layer plus yield drag alone, knocks your sales efficiency down 30%+ even if everything else stays perfect. The B2B SaaS "magic number" has dropped ~57% for exactly this reason.

Run the model BEFORE you set the quota. Not after you've missed the number.
