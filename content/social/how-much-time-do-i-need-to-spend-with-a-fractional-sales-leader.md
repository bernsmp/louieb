## LinkedIn Post (250–350 words – that refers back the to post on the website)

Six hours.

That is the number of focused hours I need from a founder in the first two weeks of any engagement.

Not sixty. Not six per week. Six total.

And almost every founder pushes back on it.

Here is the part most people get backwards. They assume hiring a Fractional Sales Leader means buying back their time on day one. They are picturing the steady state — one hour a week, a clean pipeline review, the team running the playbook without them.

That steady state is real. But it sits on the other side of an extraction.

The Sales Playbook lives in your head. Your real Ideal Customer Profile. The objections you actually hear. The questions you ask that move buyers forward. The patterns in your wins and losses. None of that is documented anywhere.

If you will not sit for the extraction, no one can build the rest. The team gets a generic playbook they ignore. And you are still selling at $5M ARR.

The curve looks like this. Weeks 1–2: four to six hours of working sessions. Weeks 3–6: two to three hours a week reviewing what I produce. Weeks 7–12: one to two hours a week, mostly the standing pipeline review. Month four onward: thirty to sixty minutes a week.

Thirty hours of founder time across 90 days. About half of one working week. To install a system that runs for years.

The founders who win this engagement are the ones who block six hours in their calendar like they would a board meeting. The ones who hide for two weeks end up with another year of founder-led sales.

Front-loading is the trade. Treat the first two weeks like the most important strategic work of the quarter — because it is.
